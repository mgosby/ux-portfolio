UX Work

Dryad Data Submission System

Dryad is an open source digital data repository where researchers upload and submit the data that is associated with their publication. Fees for uploading data will be introduced and the data submission process needs to be updated to inform researchers of the charges and to collect payment.

I interviewed potential and current Dryad users, designed the checkout process, and conducted usability testing. My deliverables included: sketches, interview notes, interaction diagrams, wireframes , and the usability testing report (PDF, 168kb) .

Trait Database

Working groups made up of computational biologists and researchers requested a database where they can store, retrieve, and share their trait data. I planned and conducted the requirements gathering workshop with the researchers, designed the upload interface, and wrote the documentation for Trait Database .

Curator Notes

Dryad curators are responsible for reviewing, archiving, and managing the data that researchers upload to the Dryad Web site. My task was to design a system that allowed for Dryad curators to communicate with one another within the Dryad system, instead of through a spreadsheet. Using an iterative process, I interviewed curators, created wireframes, mockups, and an Axure prototype , and conducted usability testing with the curators using the prototypes.

Go NAP SACC

Go NAP SACC is a program that helps child care centers improve their physical activity and nutrition practices. In order to transition this program from paper-based to online, I created a site map and conducted card sorting with current users. I also created wireframes and paper prototypes for some of the internal pages of the Web site. Lastly, I created the usability testing plan and walked the team through the process of conducting usability testing sessions.
